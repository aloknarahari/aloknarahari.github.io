—About Me—
Resume

I recently graduated from U.C. with a degree in Economics and I am eager to put my skills and experiences to use.

I'm interested in analyzing the multitude of factors that shape our society in order to influence decisions on how to improve the community. I'm paricularly interested in exploring data for clues on why things are the way they are and how to improve them. Consequently, in the past few years I've developed my skills plumbing data in order to try and identify the signal from the noise.

In college I worked as a news reporter for the Daily Californian where I experienced the hectic yet rewarding world of journalism. I also co-founded a branch of 180 Degrees Consulting, an international pro-bono consultancy, here at Berkeley to help out the plethora of nonprofit institutions in the Bay.

I've worked at a variety of organizations including a startup, a city council, and a chamber of commerce. I've also conducted research on economic clusters with a research group who seek to create well-paying jobs in the energy sector. Although diverse, these experiences are all linked by my underlying desire to impact policy in a notable way.
