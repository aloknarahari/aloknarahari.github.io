My name's Alok (pronounced uh-LOAK) and I'm an Economics Major at UC Berkeley. My passion lies in learning about the idiosyncratic and often random world we live in through data, literature, and experience. I strive to harness this dynamic knowledge and make a tangible impact in whatever industry I enter.

BIO

As a third year student, I am excited to graduate in December and apply the quantitative and qualitative skills I acquired in college.

I find economics particularly interesting as it allows me to use math and statistics to address and comprehend a litany of issues. Supplementarily, in the past few years I've developed a passion for analyzing data and trying to identify the signal from the noise.

I currently work as a news reporter for the Daily Californian where I have experienced the hectic yet rewarding world of journalism. I also co-founded a branch of 180 Degrees Consulting, an international pro-bono consultancy, here at Berkeley to help out the plethora of nonprofit organizations in the Bay.

In the past, I've worked at a variety of organizations including a startup, a city council, and a chamber of commerce. Although diverse, they are all linked by my underlying desire to impact policy in a notable way.
